# Sponsor OS — the 3-minute demo

For showing the committee, faculty advisors, or next year's team. Works on a phone.
Prep: be logged in as a `sponsorship` user; have one 🧪 demo lead visible.

---

**0:00 — The problem** (say it, don't show it)
> "Every year we start sponsorship from zero: no list, no prices, no memory of what
> worked. Sponsor OS is the chapter's memory. It costs ₹0 to run."

**0:20 — Lead Board**
Open the Lead Board. Point at the Evidence Score column.
> "Every Monday it scrapes rival fests and news for brands that sponsor events like
> ours. This score isn't an AI guess — tap any row…"

**0:45 — The proof**
Tap a high-score lead. Show the evidence list, tap **View source ↗**.
> "…every score links to public proof. If a brand asks 'why us?', we show them
> where we found them."

**1:10 — Generate a deck**
Tap **Generate Deck** (use a 🧪 demo lead — note the practice watermark). Watch the
stages: researching → colors → story → slides.
> "Thirty seconds: it researches the brand, picks up their colors, writes an
> evidence-backed story, and prices our tiers with honest ranges — never fake
> certainty."

**1:50 — Download + email**
Show the PPTX download and the editable email. Point at the caption.
> "And the rule printed right there: review and edit before sending. There is no
> send button in this entire app. Sending is a human act."

**2:20 — Close the loop**
Go to **Outcomes**, tap **💬 Reply** on the demo lead.
> "After every pitch, one tap. That's the whole ask."

**2:35 — The payoff**
Go to **Home**: the funnel and the progress bar.
> "Those taps feed a funnel the committee sees live, and at 50 real outcomes the
> system starts learning which brands convert — and reuses the language from
> pitches that won. The more we use it, the better it pitches. It remembers, so
> the next committee doesn't start from zero."

**3:00 — done.**

---

One-line answers for likely questions:
- *"What does it cost?"* — ₹0. Free tiers only, by design; rejecting paid plans is a rule.
- *"What if the AI makes something up?"* — Extracted brands must appear verbatim in
  the source page; deck claims must cite stored evidence; rejected otherwise.
- *"Who can use it?"* — Anyone with an invite code; roles limit what they can touch.
- *"What happens when you graduate?"* — Chapter-owned accounts + a 30-minute
  handover ritual in the README. That's the point of the whole system.
